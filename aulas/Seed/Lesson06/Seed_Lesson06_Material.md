## üîß ESTRUTURAS LINGU√çSTICAS

### 1. Adjectives: Color + Noun

**Structure:** COLOR + NOUN

In English, adjectives (like colors) come BEFORE the noun.

**Pattern:**
```
a RED shirt
a BLUE dress
GREEN pants
YELLOW shoes
```

**Examples:**
- I have a **red** shirt.
- She wears a **blue** dress.
- He has **black** shoes.
- I like **green** pants.

**Important Notes:**
- Adjectives DON'T change for plural:
  - ‚úì red shirt ‚Üí red shirt**s** (red stays same)
  - ‚úó reds shirts (WRONG)
  
- Color adjectives don't change with gender:
  - a red shirt (masculine)
  - a red dress (feminine)
  - red shoes (plural)

**Multiple Adjectives:**
You can use more than one adjective:
- a **big red** shirt
- **new black** shoes
- a **beautiful blue** dress

**Practice Pattern:**
- "I see a ___ ___." (color + item)
- "I see a **red** **shirt**."
- "I see **blue** **pants**."

---

### 2. Present Continuous: "I'm wearing..."

**Function:** Describe what someone is wearing RIGHT NOW

**Structure:** Subject + AM/IS/ARE + WEARING + clothes

#### Affirmative Form

**I/You/We/They:**
- I **am wearing** a shirt. (I'm wearing)
- You **are wearing** jeans. (You're wearing)
- We **are wearing** hats. (We're wearing)
- They **are wearing** shoes. (They're wearing)

**He/She/It:**
- He **is wearing** a jacket. (He's wearing)
- She **is wearing** a dress. (She's wearing)
- It **is wearing** a collar. (It's wearing - for animals)

**Contractions (Common in Speaking):**
- I am ‚Üí I'm
- You are ‚Üí You're
- He is ‚Üí He's
- She is ‚Üí She's
- We are ‚Üí We're
- They are ‚Üí They're

**Full Examples:**
- I'm wearing **a blue shirt**.
- She's wearing **a red dress**.
- They're wearing **black shoes**.
- He's wearing **jeans and a t-shirt**.

---

#### Negative Form

**Structure:** Subject + AM/IS/ARE + NOT + WEARING

**Examples:**
- I'**m not wearing** a hat.
- He'**s not wearing** (He **isn't wearing**) a jacket.
- They'**re not wearing** (They **aren't wearing**) shoes.

**Two Forms for Negatives:**
- He **is not** wearing = He **isn't** wearing
- They **are not** wearing = They **aren't** wearing

---

#### Question Form

**Structure:** AM/IS/ARE + subject + WEARING...?

**Questions:**
- **Are** you **wearing** a jacket?
- **Is** he **wearing** jeans?
- **Are** they **wearing** shoes?

**Short Answers:**
- Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.
- Yes, he is. / No, he isn't.
- Yes, they are. / No, they aren't.

**Question Word Questions:**
- **What** are you wearing?
- **What** is she wearing?
- **What color** is your shirt?

---

### 3. Describing What Others Wear

**Pattern:** He/She + IS WEARING + color + item

**Examples:**
- **Maria is wearing a red dress.**
- **Jo√£o is wearing blue jeans.**
- **My teacher is wearing a black jacket.**
- **The student is wearing white shoes.**

**With Colors:**
- He's wearing a **green** shirt.
- She's wearing **yellow** pants.
- They're wearing **brown** boots.

**Multiple Items:**
- He's wearing **a shirt and pants**.
- She's wearing **a dress and shoes**.
- I'm wearing **jeans, a t-shirt, and sneakers**.

---

### 4. Articles with Clothes

**Singular Items - Use A/AN:**
- a shirt
- a dress
- a hat
- an outfit

**Plural Items - No A/AN:**
- pants (always plural)
- jeans (always plural)
- shoes (pair)
- socks (pair)
- shorts (always plural)

**Examples:**
- I'm wearing **a** shirt. (singular)
- I'm wearing **pants**. (plural, no "a")
- I'm wearing **jeans**. (plural, no "a")
- She's wearing **a** dress. (singular)
- He's wearing **shoes**. (plural, no "a")

**Special Note:**
Some clothing items are ALWAYS plural in English:
- pants, jeans, shorts, glasses

Say: "I'm wearing **black pants**" (NOT "a black pants")

**Variations:**
- **1-2 students:** Teacher vs student, take turns calling colors
- **3-5 students:** Competitive hunt, keep score
- **Add clothes:** "Touch someone wearing RED!"

**Language Practice:**
- Students must SAY what they found: "A red book!" "Blue pants!"
- Practice: "This is ___" and "I see ___"

## üìñ CONTE√öDO DA LI√á√ÉO (LESSON CONTENT)

### PART 1: Colors Vocabulary (10 min)

**Step 1: Introduction with Visuals (4 min)**
- Display color cards or colored objects
- Say each color clearly, students repeat 3x
- Point to objects in classroom:
  - "This is RED. Like this book."
  - "This is BLUE. Like the sky."

**Step 2: Color Recognition Game (3 min)**
- Teacher points to objects
- Students shout the color
- Speed round: faster and faster!

**Step 3: Personal Items (3 min)**
- Students identify colors of their own items:
  - "My bag is black."
  - "My pen is blue."
  - "My phone is white."

---

### PART 2: Clothing Vocabulary (12 min)

**Step 1: Body Categories (4 min)**
- Organize by body part:
  - **Head:** hat, cap
  - **Upper body:** shirt, t-shirt, jacket, sweater
  - **Lower body:** pants, jeans, skirt, shorts
  - **Feet:** shoes, sneakers, boots, socks

**Step 2: Visual Introduction (4 min)**
- Show pictures or point to actual clothes
- Students repeat each word 3x
- Practice pronunciation:
  - **SHIRT** (not "shert")
  - **PANTS** (always plural)
  - **JEANS** (J sound, always plural)

**Step 3: "I have..." Statements (4 min)**
- Students list clothes they own:
  - "I have a blue shirt."
  - "I have black shoes."
  - "I have jeans."

---

### PART 3: Combining Colors and Clothes (8 min)

**Step 1: Pattern Practice (3 min)**
- Model: COLOR + ITEM
  - "a red shirt"
  - "blue jeans"
  - "black shoes"
- Students repeat patterns

**Step 2: Description Game (3 min)**
- Teacher describes a student: "I see someone wearing a blue shirt and black pants."
- Class guesses who it is
- Take turns describing

**Step 3: Personal Description (2 min)**
- Each student describes one item they're wearing:
  - "I have a red t-shirt."
  - "I have blue jeans."

---

### PART 4: Present Continuous "I'm wearing..." (15 min)

**Step 1: Structure Introduction (5 min)**
- Write on board:

- Explain: Use this to say what you're wearing RIGHT NOW

**Step 2: Teacher Models (3 min)**
- Teacher describes own outfit:
  - "I'm wearing a blue shirt."
  - "I'm wearing black pants."
  - "I'm wearing brown shoes."
- Point to each item while speaking

**Step 3: Students Describe Themselves (4 min)**
- Each student says 2-3 sentences:
  - "I'm wearing a white t-shirt."
  - "I'm wearing blue jeans."
  - "I'm wearing white sneakers."

**Step 4: Describing Others (3 min)**
- Students describe classmates or teacher:
  - "Maria is wearing a red dress."

## üåü DICAS CULTURAIS

**Clothing Culture Around the World:**

1. **Dress Codes:**
   - **USA/UK:** Casual dress is common (jeans + t-shirt)
   - **Work:** Business casual or suits depending on job
   - **School:** Many countries have school uniforms

2. **Weather and Clothing:**
   - Cold countries: People wear lots of layers
   - "It's better to overdress than underdress"
   - Americans: casual even in cold weather (shorts in winter!)

3. **Fashion Expressions:**
   - "You look good!" = compliment on appearance
   - "I like your outfit!" = compliment on clothes
   - "That suits you!" = that looks good on you
   - "Dress to impress" = wear nice clothes

4. **Shoe Culture:**
   - In USA/UK: People often wear shoes inside houses
   - In many Asian countries: Take off shoes at door
   - Sneakers are very popular worldwide

5. **Color Meanings:**
   - **Black:** Formal, elegant, professional
   - **White:** Clean, pure (wedding dress)
   - **Red:** Bold, confident, passionate
   - **Blue:** Trust, calm (popular for business)
