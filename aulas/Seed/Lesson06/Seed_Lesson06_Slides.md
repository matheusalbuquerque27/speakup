## SLIDE 11: PRESENT CONTINUOUS - INTRODUCTION
### "I'm wearing..." Structure

**Visual:** Person getting dressed with labels

**New Grammar: PRESENT CONTINUOUS**
Use this to describe what you're wearing RIGHT NOW

**Structure:**
```
Subject + AM/IS/ARE + WEARING + clothes

I AM WEARING a shirt.
You ARE WEARING jeans.
He IS WEARING shoes.
She IS WEARING a dress.
```

**Contractions:**
- I am = I'm
- You are = You're
- He is = He's
- She is = She's

**Examples:**
- I**'m wearing** a blue shirt.
- You**'re wearing** jeans.
- He**'s wearing** a jacket.

## SLIDE 18: ARTICLES WITH CLOTHES
### When to Use A/AN

**Visual:** Chart showing singular vs plural clothing

**Rule:**

**Singular Items - Use A/AN:**
```
A shirt
A dress
A jacket
AN outfit
```

**Plural Items - NO A/AN:**
```
Pants (NOT "a pants")
Jeans (NOT "a jeans")
Shoes (NOT "a shoes")
Socks (NOT "a socks")
Shorts (NOT "a shorts")
```

**Why?**
Some clothes are ALWAYS PLURAL in English:
- pants, jeans, shorts (two legs)
- shoes, socks (pair)
- glasses (pair of lenses)

**Correct Examples:**
- I'm wearing **a** shirt. ‚úì
- I'm wearing **pants**. ‚úì (NO "a")
- I'm wearing **jeans**. ‚úì (NO "a")

## SLIDE 24: CULTURAL NOTE
### Clothes Around the World

**Visual:** People from different countries in traditional/modern clothes

**Discussion Points:**

**1. Dress Codes:**
- USA/UK: Very casual (jeans everywhere!)
- Brazil: Also casual but more stylish
- Some countries: More formal dress

**2. Weather Differences:**
- Cold countries: Lots of layers, coats, boots
- Warm countries: Light clothes, shorts, t-shirts

**3. Fashion Culture:**
- Compliments: "I like your outfit!"
- Common: Jeans are popular worldwide
- Sneakers: Very popular in all countries

**4. Fun Differences:**
- Americans wear shoes inside houses (usually)
- Some Asian countries: Remove shoes at door
- School uniforms common in UK, Japan, Brazil

**Question:**
"What clothes are popular in Brazil but not in other countries?"
