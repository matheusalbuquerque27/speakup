### 2. Negative Form (Forma Negativa)

**Structure:** Subject + do/does + not + base verb

**I/You/We/They + DON'T + base verb**
- I **don't** wake up early.
- You **don't** study at night.
- We **don't** work on Sundays.
- They **don't** eat breakfast.

**He/She/It + DOESN'T + base verb**
- He **doesn't** wake up early.
- She **doesn't** study at night.
- It **doesn't** work.

**Key Points:**
- DON'T = do not
- DOESN'T = does not
- After DON'T/DOESN'T, use BASE VERB (no -s)
- ‚úì He doesn't work. (CORRECT)
- ‚úó He doesn't works. (WRONG)

---

### 3. Interrogative Form (Forma Interrogativa)

**Structure:** Do/Does + subject + base verb...?

**DO + I/you/we/they + base verb...?**
- **Do** you wake up early?
- **Do** they study English?
- **Do** we work tomorrow?

**DOES + he/she/it + base verb...?**
- **Does** he wake up early?
- **Does** she study English?
- **Does** it work well?

**Short Answers:**
- Yes, I do. / No, I don't.
- Yes, he does. / No, he doesn't.
- Yes, they do. / No, they don't.

**Question Words + Do/Does:**
- **What time** do you wake up?
- **When** does she study?
- **Where** do they work?

**Procedimento:**

   - One student mimes 3 actions in order
   - Others identify the sequence: "You wake up, you shower, you eat breakfast!"

- **1-2 students:** Student mimes, teacher guesses or helps
- **3-5 students:** Take turns, group guesses together
- **Add Time:** "I wake up at 7 AM!" (practice telling time)

## üìñ CONTE√öDO DA LI√á√ÉO (LESSON CONTENT)

### PART 1: Introduction to Daily Routine Vocabulary (15 min)

**Step 1: Visual Introduction**
- Show pictures or act out each routine action
- Say each word clearly: "Wake up" (repeat 3x)
- Students repeat in chorus

**Step 2: Timeline of a Day**
- Draw a simple timeline on board: Morning ‚Üí Afternoon ‚Üí Evening ‚Üí Night
- Place routine activities on timeline:
  - Morning: wake up, shower, breakfast
  - Afternoon: lunch, work, study
  - Evening: dinner
  - Night: watch TV, read, sleep

**Step 3: Personal Example**
- Teacher describes own routine:
  - "I wake up at 6 AM."
  - "I eat breakfast at 7 AM."
  - "I work from 8 AM to 5 PM."
  - "I sleep at 11 PM."

---

### PART 2: Simple Present - Third Person -S (20 min)

**Step 1: Discovery**
- Write on board:
  - I wake up ‚Üí He wake**s** up
  - I study ‚Üí She stud**ies**
  - I have breakfast ‚Üí He **has** breakfast
  
**Step 2: Rules Explanation**
- Most verbs: add -S (work ‚Üí works)
- Verbs ending in -ch, -sh, -s, -x, -o: add -ES (watch ‚Üí watches)
- Verbs ending in consonant + Y: change to -IES (study ‚Üí studies)
- Irregular: have ‚Üí has

**Step 3: Guided Practice**
- Transform sentences together:
  - "I work." ‚Üí "He ___." (works)
  - "I study." ‚Üí "She ___." (studies)
  - "I watch TV." ‚Üí "He ___." (watches)

**Step 4: Describing Others**
- Students describe family member's routine:
  - "My father works every day."
  - "My mother wakes up at 6 AM."
  - "My brother studies at night."

---

### PART 3: Time Expressions (15 min)

**Step 1: Prepositions of Time**
- Teach IN / AT / ON rules with examples
- Draw three columns on board:
  - **IN:** morning, afternoon, evening
  - **AT:** 7 AM, noon, night
  - **ON:** Monday, weekends

**Step 2: Practice Sentences**
- Complete together:
  - "I wake up ___ the morning." (in)
  - "I wake up ___ 7 AM." (at)
  - "I don't work ___ Sundays." (on)

**Step 3: Personalization**
- Students create sentences about their routine with time expressions:
  - "I wake up at 7 AM in the morning."
  - "I study at night."
  - "I don't work on weekends."

---

### PART 4: Questions and Negatives (10 min)

**Step 1: Question Formation**
- Model: "Do you wake up early?"
- Teach: Do/Does + subject + base verb?
- Practice Q&A in pairs

**Step 2: Negative Sentences**
- Model: "I don't work on Sundays."
- Teach: don't/doesn't + base verb
- Students make negative sentences about themselves
