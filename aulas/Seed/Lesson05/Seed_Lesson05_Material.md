## üîß ESTRUTURAS LINGU√çSTICAS

### 1. Expressing Likes and Dislikes

#### Structure: LIKE / DON'T LIKE

**Affirmative:**
- I **like** apples.
- You **like** coffee.
- We **like** pizza.
- They **like** vegetables.

**He/She/It:**
- He **likes** bananas.
- She **likes** juice.
- It **likes** fish. (for animals)

**Negative:**
- I **don't like** fish.
- You **don't like** coffee.
- We **don't like** salad.
- They **don't like** meat.

**He/She/It:**
- He **doesn't like** milk.
- She **doesn't like** vegetables.

**Key Points:**
- Use LIKE with I/you/we/they
- Use LIKES with he/she/it (add -S)
- Negative: don't like / doesn't like
- After LIKE, use noun: "I like coffee" (not "I like the coffee")

---

### 2. Questions with "Do you like...?"

**Question Structure:**

**Do/Does + subject + like + food/drink?**

**With I/You/We/They:**
- **Do** you like pizza?
- **Do** they like coffee?
- **Do** we like vegetables?

**With He/She/It:**
- **Does** he like chicken?
- **Does** she like juice?
- **Does** your dog like fish?

**Short Answers:**
- Yes, I do. / No, I don't.
- Yes, she does. / No, she doesn't.
- Yes, they do. / No, they don't.

**Question Word Questions:**
- **What** do you like?
- **What** does she like?
- **What's** your favorite food?

**Example Exchanges:**
```
A: Do you like pizza?
B: Yes, I do! I love pizza!

A: Does your brother like vegetables?
B: No, he doesn't. He doesn't like vegetables.

A: What do you like for breakfast?
B: I like bread and coffee.
```

### 3. Countable vs Uncountable Nouns (Basic Introduction)

**Countable Nouns** (Substantivos Cont√°veis)
- You can count them: 1 apple, 2 apples, 3 apples
- They have singular and plural forms
- Use A/AN before singular countable nouns

**Examples:**
- **an** apple ‚Üí two apple**s**
- **a** banana ‚Üí three banana**s**
- **an** egg ‚Üí four egg**s**
- **an** orange ‚Üí five orange**s**

**Uncountable Nouns** (Substantivos Incont√°veis)
- You cannot count them individually
- They don't have plural forms
- DON'T use A/AN before them
- Use "some" or no article

**Examples:**
- water (NOT: a water, two waters ‚ùå)
- rice (NOT: a rice, three rices ‚ùå)
- bread (NOT: a bread ‚ùå)
- milk (NOT: a milk ‚ùå)
- coffee (NOT: a coffee ‚ùå in general; but "a coffee" = one cup ‚úì)
- juice (NOT: a juice ‚ùå in general; but "a juice" = one glass ‚úì)

**How to Express Quantity with Uncountable:**
- a glass of water
- a cup of coffee
- a bowl of rice
- a slice of bread
- a piece of cheese

**Common Patterns:**

| Countable | Uncountable |
|-----------|-------------|
| an apple | water |
| a banana | milk |
| an orange | rice |
| an egg | bread |
| a vegetable | cheese |
| a chicken (whole bird) | chicken (meat) |
| a fish (whole) | fish (meat) |

### 4. Articles: A / AN

**Rule:**
- Use **A** before consonant sounds
- Use **AN** before vowel sounds (a, e, i, o, u)

**Examples with A:**
- **a** banana
- **a** pizza
- **a** sandwich
- **a** cup

**Examples with AN:**
- **an** apple
- **an** orange
- **an** egg
- **an** ice cream

**Practice:**
- I eat **a** banana every day.
- I want **an** apple.
- She drinks **a** glass of water.
- He eats **an** egg for breakfast.

**Remember:**
- It's about the SOUND, not the letter
- **a** university (sounds like "yu")
- **an** hour (h is silent)

## üéÆ ATIVIDADE DE AQUECIMENTO (WARM-UP)

### Food Flashcard Race

**Dura√ß√£o:** 10 minutos  
**Objetivo:** Ativar vocabul√°rio de comidas e criar energia positiva

**Materiais:**
- Flashcards com imagens de comidas e bebidas (20-30 cards)
- Timer (opcional)

**Procedimento:**

**Setup (2 min):**
- Place flashcards face down on table or scattered in room
- Divide class into teams (if 2+ students) or work individually

**Round 1: Speed Recognition (3 min)**
1. Teacher shows flashcard quickly
2. Students shout the word in English
3. First to say correctly gets a point
4. Go through 15-20 cards

**Round 2: Memory Challenge (3 min)**
1. Lay 10-12 cards face up for 30 seconds
2. Students look and try to remember
3. Turn cards face down
4. Call out a food: "Apple!"
5. Students race to find the apple card
6. Winner says: "I like apples!" or "I don't like apples!"

**Round 3: Category Sort (2 min)**
- Students sort cards into categories:
  - Fruits
  - Vegetables
  - Drinks
  - Meat
- Practice saying: "Apples are fruits. Chicken is meat."

**Variations:**
- **1 student:** Teacher vs student, take turns
- **2-3 students:** Compete against each other
- **4-5 students:** Team game

## üìñ CONTE√öDO DA LI√á√ÉO (LESSON CONTENT)

### PART 1: Food and Drink Vocabulary (12 min)

**Step 1: Visual Introduction (5 min)**
- Display pictures of food items
- Say each word clearly, students repeat 3x
- Group by category:
  - **Fruits:** apple, banana, orange
  - **Vegetables:** salad, vegetables
  - **Carbs:** bread, rice, pasta
  - **Proteins:** chicken, fish, egg
  - **Drinks:** water, juice, milk, coffee

**Step 2: Pronunciation Practice (3 min)**
- Focus on tricky words:
  - **vegetables** (VEG-tuh-buhls)
  - **juice** (not "juicy")
  - **chicken** (CH sound)
  - **breakfast** (BREK-fust)

**Step 3: Personal Connection (4 min)**
- Teacher models: "I like pizza. I don't like fish."
- Students think about their favorites
- Quick poll: "Who likes pizza? Raise your hand!"

---

### PART 2: "I like / I don't like" (15 min)

**Step 1: Structure Introduction (5 min)**
- Write on board:
  ```
  ‚úì I LIKE coffee.
  ‚úó I DON'T LIKE milk.
  ```
- Explain: Use this to talk about preferences
- Model multiple examples
- Students repeat chorally

**Step 2: Third Person (5 min)**
- Show transformation:
  ```
  I like ‚Üí He LIKES
  I don't like ‚Üí He DOESN'T LIKE
  ```
- Practice conjugation:
  - "I like pizza." ‚Üí "She likes pizza."
  - "I don't like fish." ‚Üí "He doesn't like fish."

**Step 3: Personal Expression (5 min)**
- Each student says 3 sentences:
  - 2 things they like
  - 1 thing they don't like
- Example: "I like rice. I like chicken. I don't like fish."
- Teacher writes sentences on board for visual support

---

### PART 3: Questions "Do you like...?" (15 min)

**Step 1: Question Formation (5 min)**
- Write structure:
  ```
  DO you LIKE pizza?
  DOES she LIKE coffee?
  ```
- Practice transforming statements to questions:
  - "You like pizza." ‚Üí "Do you like pizza?"
  - "She likes coffee." ‚Üí "Does she like coffee?"

**Step 2: Short Answers (4 min)**
- Teach response patterns:
  ```
  Q: Do you like pizza?
  A: Yes, I do. / No, I don't.
  
  Q: Does he like coffee?
  A: Yes, he does. / No, he doesn't.
  ```
- Drill with multiple questions

**Step 3: Interview Practice (6 min)**
- Students interview each other (or teacher interviews if solo)
- Questions:
  - "Do you like pizza?"
  - "Do you like vegetables?"
  - "Do you like coffee?"
  - "What's your favorite food?"
- Record answers to share with class

---

### PART 4: Countable vs Uncountable (8 min)

**Step 1: Introduction to Concept (3 min)**
- Explain: Some nouns you can count, some you can't
- **Countable:** 1 apple, 2 apples (use a/an)
- **Uncountable:** water, rice, bread (no a/an)

**Step 2: Sort Activity (3 min)**
- Create two columns on board: COUNTABLE | UNCOUNTABLE
- Sort words together:
  - **Countable:** apple, banana, egg, orange
  - **Uncountable:** water, rice, bread, milk, coffee

**Step 3: Practice with A/AN (2 min)**
- Practice sentences:
  - "I want **an** apple."
  - "I drink **some** water." (not "a water")
  - "I eat **a** banana."
  - "I like **rice**." (no article)
