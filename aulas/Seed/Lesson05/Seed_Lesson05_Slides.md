## SLIDE 2: WARM-UP - FOOD FLASHCARD RACE
### Activity: Flashcard Race

**Visual:** Flashcards spread out or stacked

**Instructions:**
1. Spread 20-30 food/drink flashcards on table
2. Teacher shows one card quickly
3. Students shout the name in English
4. First correct answer gets a point

**Round 1: Speed Recognition (3 min)**
- Show cards quickly
- Students identify: "Apple!" "Banana!" "Pizza!"

**Round 2: Memory Game (4 min)**
- Show 10 cards for 30 seconds
- Flip face down
- Call out: "Find the apple!"
- Winner says: "I like apples!" or "I don't like apples!"

**Round 3: Category Sort (3 min)**
- Sort cards: Fruits | Vegetables | Drinks | Meat

## SLIDE 9: STRUCTURE - THIRD PERSON
### He/She LIKES

**Visual:** Transformation chart

**Grammar Rule:**
```
I LIKE ‚Üí He LIKES
I LIKE ‚Üí She LIKES
I DON'T LIKE ‚Üí He DOESN'T LIKE
```

**Examples:**
- I like pizza. ‚Üí **She likes pizza.**
- I like coffee. ‚Üí **He likes coffee.**
- I don't like fish. ‚Üí **He doesn't like fish.**

**Key Point:**
- Add **-S** to verb with he/she/it
- Use **DOESN'T** (not don't) with he/she/it

**Practice:**
Transform together:
- "I like apples." ‚Üí "My mother ___." (likes apples)
- "I don't like vegetables." ‚Üí "My brother ___." (doesn't like vegetables)

## SLIDE 13: COUNTABLE VS UNCOUNTABLE - INTRODUCTION
### Two Types of Nouns

**Visual:** Two columns with examples

**COUNTABLE (Cont√°veis)**
- You can count them!
- Have singular and plural
- Use A/AN

**Examples:**
```
1 apple, 2 apples, 3 apples
1 banana, 2 bananas
1 egg, 2 eggs
```

**UNCOUNTABLE (Incont√°veis)**
- You CANNOT count them
- No plural form
- NO A/AN

**Examples:**
```
water (NOT: a water ‚ùå)
rice (NOT: two rices ‚ùå)
bread (NOT: a bread ‚ùå)
milk (NOT: three milks ‚ùå)
```

**Teacher Explains:**
- "Can you count apples? Yes! 1 apple, 2 apples."
- "Can you count water? No! You say 'water', not 'one water'."

## SLIDE 15: ARTICLES - A / AN
### Using A and AN

**Visual:** Rule chart with examples

**Rule:**
- Use **A** before consonant sounds
- Use **AN** before vowel sounds (a, e, i, o, u)

**A + Consonant:**
```
A banana
A pizza
A sandwich
A cup
```

**AN + Vowel:**
```
AN apple
AN orange
AN egg
AN ice cream
```

**Practice:**
Fill in A or AN:
1. ___ apple (an)
2. ___ banana (a)
3. ___ egg (an)
4. ___ pizza (a)
5. ___ orange (an)

## SLIDE 18: GAME - FOOD BINGO
### Let's Play Bingo!

**Visual:** Bingo card template with food images

**Setup:**
- Give each student a bingo card with 9-16 food pictures
- Cards should have different arrangements

**How to Play:**
1. Teacher calls out foods: "Apple!" "Pizza!" "Water!"
2. Students mark their cards if they have that food
3. First to complete a row/column/diagonal shouts "BINGO!"
4. Winner must say: "I like ___" for each food in their winning line

**Language Focus:**
- Practice food vocabulary
- Practice "I like..." structure
- Listening comprehension

**Variations:**
- Call out sentences: "I like apples!" (students find "apple")
- Call out questions: "Do you like pizza?" (students find "pizza")
