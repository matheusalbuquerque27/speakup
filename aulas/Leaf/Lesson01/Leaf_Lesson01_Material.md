## üéØ Summary of Linguistic Structures

### 1. Modal Verbs for Advice

| Modal Verb | Use | Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|------------|-----|-------------|----------|----------|
| **Should** | General advice | You should exercise. | You shouldn't smoke. | Should I go? |
| **Ought to** | Formal advice | You ought to study. | You ought not to lie. | Ought I to wait? |
| **Could** | Gentle suggestion | You could try yoga. | You couldn't do that. | Could I help? |
| **Had better** | Strong advice/warning | You'd better hurry. | You'd better not be late. | ‚Äî |

**Examples in Context:**
- **Should:** "You should see a dentist if your tooth hurts."
- **Ought to:** "Students ought to arrive on time for class."
- **Could:** "You could learn guitar online. There are many free lessons."
- **Had better:** "You'd better save some money for emergencies, or you'll have problems later."

---

### 2. Making Suggestions - Question Forms

| Expression | Structure | Example |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| **Why don't you...?** | Why don't you + base verb? | Why don't you take a break? |
| **Why doesn't he/she...?** | Why doesn't + subject + base verb? | Why doesn't he apply for the job? |
| **How about...?** | How about + gerund/noun? | How about going to the park? |
| **What about...?** | What about + gerund/noun? | What about trying meditation? |
| **What if...?** | What if + subject + verb? | What if we start tomorrow? |

**Examples in Context:**
- A: "I'm feeling stressed about work."  
  B: "**Why don't you** take a short vacation?"

- A: "We need to celebrate Maria's birthday."  
  B: "**How about organizing** a surprise party?"

- A: "I want to save money but I don't know how."  
  B: "**What if you** open a savings account?"

---

### 3. Strong Advice - Second Conditional

**Structure:** If I were you, I would + base verb

This structure is used to give personal advice by imagining yourself in the other person's situation.

**Examples:**
- "**If I were you, I would** accept that job offer. It's a great opportunity."
- "**If I were you, I wouldn't** worry so much. Everything will be fine."
- "**If I were in your position, I'd** talk to the manager about it."
- "**If I were in your shoes, I would** apologize and explain what happened."

**Note:** We use "were" (not "was") for all persons in the subjunctive mood, though "If I was you" is also common in informal speech.
